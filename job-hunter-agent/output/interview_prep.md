# Interview Prep: Beta Inc – Junior Data Analyst

## Job Overview
Beta Inc is hiring a full-time Junior Data Analyst in New York, NY (hybrid/remote-friendly) to support marketing and sales teams. You will:

- Clean, validate, and standardize raw campaign and CRM datasets  
- Build ad-hoc reports and dashboards (Tableau, Excel) to track KPIs and campaign performance  
- Collaborate closely with marketing, sales, and data engineering to translate insights into action  
- Use SQL, Python (Pandas), and Excel daily  
- Report into a Senior Analytics Manager as part of a 5-person analytics pod  

Compensation: \$55–65 K/year, benefits include remote days, health coverage, and stock options.

## Why This Job Is a Fit
- You have 3 years of hands-on SQL, Python, and Excel experience—exceeding the 1 year minimum  
- Proven track record cleaning/validating large data (>500 K rows) and reducing errors by up to 25%  
- Extensive ad-hoc reporting background (Tableau, Power BI, Excel) that drove 12–15% performance lifts for marketing campaigns  
- Prior cross-functional collaboration with marketing and sales aligns with Beta’s customer-centric, team-based approach  
- Hybrid work style suits both your NJ location and Beta’s flexible policy

1. **Technical – SQL & Python**  
   - “How would you write a SQL query to identify duplicate records and remove them?”  
   - “Walk me through your process for cleaning missing or inconsistent values in a Pandas DataFrame.”  
2. **Excel & BI Tools**  
   - “Describe how you built a pivot table to analyze campaign ROI.”  
   - “Which chart types do you choose for time-series vs. categorical comparisons?”  
3. **Analytics & Reporting**  
   - “How do you define and track key marketing KPIs: CAC, LTV, conversion rate?”  
   - “Explain a time you created an ad-hoc report under tight deadlines.”  
4. **Cross-Functional Collaboration**  
   - “Tell me about a project where you partnered with sales/marketing to influence strategy.”  
   - “How do you communicate technical findings to non-technical stakeholders?”  
5. **Behavioral & Problem-Solving**  
   - “Describe a time you encountered messy data—what steps did you take?”  
   - “Share an example of how you handled conflicting stakeholder requirements.”

## Concepts To Know/Review
- SQL: joins (inner, left), window functions, CTEs, aggregation, deduplication strategies  
- Python & Pandas: `groupby`, `merge`, `apply`, handling nulls/outliers, vectorized operations  
- Excel: PivotTables, VLOOKUP/XLOOKUP, conditional formatting, basic macros  
- Statistics & Data Quality: central tendency, variance, data validation checks, anomaly detection  
- BI & Reporting: dashboard design best practices, data storytelling frameworks (e.g., STAR)  
- Marketing Analytics: CAC, LTV, attribution modeling, A/B testing fundamentals  
- Collaborative Communication: translating insights, creating executive summaries  

## Strategic Advice
- Tone: Confident, curious, collaborative—balance technical expertise with business impact  
- Focus Areas: Emphasize end-to-end process (data cleaning → analysis → storytelling → action) and cross-functional wins  
- Storytelling: Use the CAR (Context, Action, Result) framework for behavioral questions  
- Red Flags: Avoid over-focusing on code; always tie back to how insights drove business decisions  
- Logistics: Confirm hybrid schedule expectations, ask about onboarding timeline  
- Closing: Reiterate enthusiasm for Beta’s mission and how your data-driven mindset aligns with their values
